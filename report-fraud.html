Submit a Fraud Report

Please complete the form below to report any suspected fraud, unauthorized transactions, or misleading communications. This information is reviewed by our investigation team and may be referred to appropriate authorities for follow-up.

How We Review Your Report

Each submitted fraud report undergoes a multi-stage review process by authorized analysts. Upon submission, you will receive a Case ID number, which allows our system to track your submission securely. Reports are examined for authenticity, impact, and potential connection to broader incidents affecting multiple users.

Analysts prioritize cases based on urgency and the type of fraud reported. Incidents involving compromised financial information, government impersonation, or ongoing scams receive immediate attention. Data shared is strictly handled under confidentiality and is accessible only to investigative staff.

Federal Reserved Accounts works in collaboration with federal agencies including the U.S. Treasury, the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC), and the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) to ensure cross-agency coordination in fraud prevention.

Recognizing Common Fraud Schemes

Fraud schemes may take several forms — from digital phishing campaigns to identity impersonation or false claims of financial authority. Recognizing these schemes early is key to prevention:

  • Phishing Emails: Messages that mimic official correspondence but contain suspicious links or attachments.
  • Unauthorized Transactions: Withdrawals or payments from accounts without prior consent.
  • Government Impersonation: Calls or messages falsely claiming to represent federal institutions.
  • Fake Investment Offers: High-return “guaranteed” investment proposals requesting personal data.

Always verify contact sources and report questionable communication to the proper authorities through our secure channel.

Your Privacy and Data Security

Every report you submit is stored in a protected Supabase database following encryption and limited-access protocols. No report is ever shared with third parties outside the scope of investigation. We use encrypted connections for all data transmission and enforce role-based access controls.

By submitting this form, you consent to the secure storage of your information for investigation and record-keeping purposes in compliance with the Privacy Act of 1974.

Follow-Up and Case Tracking

Once a case is assigned an ID, it enters our internal tracking system. If additional information is needed, you may receive an official communication from a verified federalreservedaccounts.com email address. Be cautious of any entity requesting private account credentials — legitimate investigators will never ask for your passwords or banking PINs.

Reports remain active until resolved, and you can use your Case ID when contacting us for updates. This ensures accuracy and continuity of your investigation record.
